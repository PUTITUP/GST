CHAPTER-2

WHY WAS THERE A NEED FOR GST IN INDIA- AN INTRODUCTION TO GST COUNCIL

Before GST, INDIAN Tax System was a giant ballof numerous taxesthat were paid at different stages of business, and were seperately collected by State and Central Government. The tax rates also differed from one sate to another. Hidden taxes on exports, and no charge applicable on importing the goods from one state to another were also the instrument to fraud and forgery. This led to an enatngled tax structure, with a lot of discrepancies taking place and tax corruption getting elevated.

GST, instead, unified the tax structure and consequently the entire nation. Now, the consolidated tax collected will be divided among Central and State Government, which is an easier way to provied services and goods across the nation, without any additional taxes imposed on them.

Additional taxes imposed on goods and services at different stages meant a substanial increase in cost that the end consumer was paying. The manufacturers and traders increased the costs to even out their tax burden. Also, the complex tax structure was insufficient, with a lot of loopholes being created in the entangled web. That is the reason, a consolidated and unified text structure was needed to eliminate all these. GST Taxation will add alot of value at each stage of business and trading. Thus, the economics growth is sure to be seen, in the coming phase of GST. India can now be identified as one national market with a unified and simplified tax structure, that too at a resonable tax rate.

GST is going to bring in a lot of transparency in the entire taxation system of the country, Consumer and businessman will thus know the amount of taxes they are paying to government for manufaturing, sale and purchase of goods and services.

Now let us take a quick look at some of major benifits of GST:

SIMPLIFIED TAX STRUCTRE: As explained earlier, GST is a unified tax entity which elimates a lot of taxes that were paid earlier at different business, like VAT, Excise, Service Tax ets, which you must have seen on the bills that you recieved for your purchase. Now,GST is single tax, which is levied on any good or services being sold or purchased. This is the biggest benifit of this tax system. All the confusion is eliminated. For businesses, accounting complexities will reduce resultiong in less paperwoork, which will save both time and money. GST will increase economic GDP by 2%-2.5%.

A SUBSTANTIAL INCREASE IN TAX REVENUES: When the tax system is simplified, people will not run away from paying the taxes and willingly come forward to contribute through a single tax entity, GST. This will create a ood revenue depsite for government, to be used in further development and betterment of the nation

REDUCED PRICES OF GOODS AND SERVICES: When the taxes are reduced by removing various direct and indirect taxes, the manufaturers , traders and services providers will automatically make their offering available on lower prices for end consumers. Also, lower the prices, heigher the demand; which clearly means a substantial boost to economy. Businesses and companies will benifits from this, as they will be producing more and selling more.

A FIRM BOOST TO EXPORT INDUSTRY: With the introduction of GST, India will rise as a competitive market with a fair pricing. This will encourage more and more foreign players to enter Indian market, thus, giving a good boost to export and trading.

GST will take away cascading effect of various taxes that are charged on sale/ production/ purchase taking off the burden from all the tax payers and end consumers.

{But, all this reform making, decision making and implantaion was not easy and will not be easy in future course. To have structure in place and to make the processes smooth, a ruiling or decision making body was needed. Thus, GST Coucil came in place.

WHAT IS GST COUNCIL AND ITS FUNCTION

The key decision making body which is making all yhe important decision regarding GST, its structure the norms, regulation, enforcement and implementaion is called GST Council. It is this council that is deciding the tax rates, tex exemption, the due dates of obtaining GST forms, and deadlines of filing GST. This is council is also responsible for making decision regarding special rates and provisions for various states. The major responsibility of GST council is to ensure a uniform tax system and tax rates for good and services across the country.

HOW IS GST COUNCIL STRUCTURED

As we discussed earlier in chapter-1, Constitutional Amendement Bill 122 was introduced in the parliament to implant GST in the entire nation. This bill was passed by Rajya Sabha on 3rd August, 2016 and Lok Sabha on 08th August,2016. After being approved and accepted by 15 stated Hon'ble President Sh, Pranab Mukhherjee gave his assent to "The Constition (One Hundred And First Amendment) Act 2016" on 8th of September, 2016. After this assent, on 16th of September 2016, Government of India issued notification about bringing a council in existence, which would be solely responsible for making all decisions related to GST.

Since then GST council and been notified bringing into existence the constutional body to decide issues relating to GST. Article 279(1) of the amended Indian Constution staes that the GST Council must be to be constituted bt the President within 60 days of the commencement of the article 279A. This article also states that GST Council will be a joint forum for the Centre and States.

  1. 1. Creation of the GST Council as per Article 279A of the amended constitution.
  2. 2. Creation of the GST Council Secretariar, with its office at New Delhi.
  3. 3. Appointement of the Secretary (Revenue) as the Ex-office Secreatry to the GST Council.
  4. 4. Inclusion of the Chairperson, Central Board Of Excise and Customs(CBEC), as a permanent invitee(non-voting) to all proceeding of the GST Council.
  5. 5. To create a post for Additional Secretary to the GST Council in the GST Council Secretariat (at the level of Additional Secretary to the Government of India), and four posts of commissiner in GST Council Secretariat (at the level of Joint Secretary to the Government of India).
  6. 6. To create a post for Additional Secretary to the GST Council.
  7. 7. To create four posts of commissioner in the GST Council Secretariat.(This is at the level of Joint Secretary).
  8. 8. The cabinet also decide to provide for adequate funds for meeting the recurring and non-recurring expences of the GST Council Secretariat, the entire cost for which shall be born by the Central Government.
  9. 9. The GST Council Secretariat is manned by officers taken on deputation from both the Central and State Government.
  10. 10. GST Council Secretaruat is manned by officers taken on deputation from both the Central and State Governments.
  11. 11. The Cabinet also provides funds for meetings the expences(recurring and non-reccuring) of the GST Council Secretariat. This cost is completly borne by the Central government.
